# Competitive Signal Analysis — Audi

**Sources:** Google Trends (observed directly, June 2026); Audi Group Combined Annual and Sustainability Report 2024  
**Date:** June 2026  
**Confidence:** HIGH for Google Trends data observed directly; HIGH for Annual Report figures quoted exactly; MEDIUM for inferences and interpretations.

> **Accuracy note:** Google Trends produces relative search interest scores (0–100), not absolute search volumes. They are comparable within the same query and time window, not across different queries or time periods. All figures below are from the same query window (Portugal, past year, June 2026) and are directly comparable. Annual Report figures are audited and cited verbatim.

## 2.1 Brand Search Comparison

**Source:** Google Trends, Portugal, past year, June 2026. Search terms compared directly. Confidence: HIGH.

| Brand | Web search score | Car make entity score | vs. Audi |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------|----------|
| BMW | 86 | 86 | +40 points (web), +40 points (entity) |
| Mercedes | 73 | 78 | +28 points (web), +32 points (entity) |
| **Audi** | **45** | **46** | — |

**What this tells us:**

Audi trails BMW by 40 points and Mercedes by approximately 30 points in Portuguese search interest. In relative terms, Audi generates roughly half the search interest of BMW and 62% of Mercedes. This is a meaningful gap in a market where search interest is a leading indicator of configurator traffic and dealer contact volume.

Both the raw search term comparison and the car make entity comparison produce almost identical gaps, which rules out brand name ambiguity as a confounding factor. The signal is consistent.

**Limits of this comparison:** Google Trends measures search frequency relative to total searches, not absolute visit or lead volume. A brand with lower search interest but higher direct traffic can still outperform on conversion. This signal is directional, not definitive.

**Source:** Google Trends. Confidence: HIGH.

| Rank | Query | YoY growth | Signal type |
|------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | audi f1 team | +350% | Brand/motorsport — not purchase intent |
| 2 | audi q3 2025 | +250% | Product research — specific model year |
| 3 | novo audi q3 | +200% | Product launch interest |
| 4 | importrust | +140% | Third-party import platform — purchase intent signal |
| 5 | audi f1 | +110% | Motorsport brand interest |

| 7 | audi q3 hibrido | +90% | Hybrid variant research |
| 8 | audi q9 | +80% | Upcoming model anticipation |
| 9 | audi q4 e-tron | +60% | BEV SUV research |
| 10 | audi q3 preço | +60% | Price research — high commercial intent |

**The Q3 signal:**

The Q3 appears four times in the rising queries list (audi q3 2025, novo audi q3, audi q3, audi q3 hibrido, audi q3 preço) — a concentration of demand that makes it the dominant commercial signal in Audi's Portuguese search profile. Critically, three of these four queries are ICE or hybrid variants. The `audi q3 hibrido` query confirms the demand is for the electrified but not fully electric version. `audi q3 preço` (Q3 price) is the highest commercial-intent query in the set — users at this stage are comparison-shopping, not casually browsing.

**`importrust` at +140%:**

The appearance of a third-party import platform in Audi's rising queries is analytically significant. It suggests a portion of high-intent buyers are routing around the official Audi dealer network entirely and using grey-market import channels. This is a conversion loss that does not show up in Audi Portugal's own analytics — the leads exist; they are being captured elsewhere.

**The F1 signal:**

Audi's F1 team entry (+350%, +110% across two queries) is the single largest growth signal by percentage. This is brand awareness, not purchase intent. F1 interest drives top-of-funnel reach but does not directly translate to configurator sessions. The gap between F1 brand interest and Q3 commercial intent is an attribution problem: F1 investment is measurable in search trends but its contribution to configurator entries requires multi-touch analysis that only Audi's internal GA4 can provide.

**Observations:**

BREAKOUT status on Google Trends indicates growth of more than 5,000% from a near-zero baseline — these are new search behaviours with no prior history, not growth from an established base. Nine of the top ten YouTube rising queries achieve BREAKOUT status. This is characteristic of product launch interest rather than sustained demand.

The Q3 2026 at +2,000% is the only query with a quantified figure — suggesting it has slightly more baseline history than the pure BREAKOUT entries. The Q5 and RS5 entries confirm that YouTube interest is broader than web search, covering both volume models (Q3, Q5) and performance/aspirational models (RS5, R8).

The `audi a6 e tron sportback` BREAKOUT is the only BEV model to appear in YouTube rising queries. On YouTube, where long-form reviews and reveal videos drive discovery, BEV interest exists but is not the dominant signal relative to ICE/hybrid models.

### Google Shopping

**Source:** Google Trends — Shopping search, Portugal, past year. Confidence: HIGH.

Near zero throughout the year. An anomaly spike occurred approximately May 2026, immediately returning to zero. Pattern is identical to BMW.

**Interpretation:** Premium new cars do not transact through Google Shopping. This is expected behaviour, not a measurement anomaly. The spike likely represents a brief SEA campaign or a data artefact. It is not a signal of changed consumer behaviour.

### News Search

**Source:** Google Trends — News search, Portugal. Confidence: HIGH for the spike timing; MEDIUM for cause.

Audi news search spiked to a score of 100 during the week of May 24–31, 2026. This is the highest recorded news interest in the observed period.

**Cause: unconfirmed.** Requires verification against Portuguese automotive news sources from that period. Possible explanations include: a product announcement, a pricing change, a recall, or coverage of the F1 programme. The cause materially affects interpretation — a product launch spike is a positive commercial signal; a recall or controversy is not.

**BMW comparison:** BMW's peak news interest in the same period was driven by WSBK motorsport coverage (bmw wsbk BREAKOUT, approximately April 2026). No product recall or negative brand signals were identified for BMW.

> **Action required:** Verify the cause of Audi's May 2026 news spike before including any interpretation of it in the insight brief.

## 2.4 Audi's Stated Digital Strategy vs. Observable Signals

**Source:** Audi Group Combined Annual and Sustainability Report 2024. Confidence: HIGH — audited figures quoted directly.

| Metric | 2024 | 2023 | Change |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| Total Audi brand deliveries (global) | 1,671,218 | 1,895,240 | −11.8% |
| Europe deliveries | 670,859 | 754,549 | −11.1% |
| Best-selling model globally | Q5 — 297,912 units | — | — |
| BEVs manufactured | 158,343 | 196,761 | −19.5% |
| BEVs delivered (Brand Group Progressive) | 164,480 | 178,429 | −7.8% |
| BEV share of total production | ~9.3% | ~10.0% | −0.7pp |
| Revenue | €64,532m | €69,865m | −7.6% |

The Annual Report attributes the decline to: "challenging overall market environment, tougher competition and significantly lower overall demand for fully electric vehicles."

### What the Annual Report Does Not Contain

This is the analytically significant absence. The BMW Annual Report 2024 contained explicit statements about digital sales investment (triple-digit millions annually), online purchase rollout, and configurator as a primary conversion tool. The Audi Group Annual Report 2024 contains:

- **myAudi account** — referenced for charging portal access only
- **Configurator** — referenced for product specification only; no conversion or lead generation framing
- **No mention** of online purchase rollout, direct digital sales channel, or digital investment figures analogous to BMW's stated commitments

**What this means:**

Audi's Annual Report describes the configurator as a specification tool, not a conversion tool. BMW's frames it as a sales channel. This is not a finding about Audi's actual digital capability — internal systems are not visible from outside. It is a finding about Audi's public strategic framing: digital sales are not presented as a core commercial priority in the same way BMW presents them.

## 2.5 Analytical Implications

Four observations from the signals above that shape the audit's focus:

**1. The Q3 is the commercial signal; the e-tron models are not**

Portugal's Audi search profile is led by ICE and hybrid Q3 demand, not by BEV models. The Q4 e-tron appears at +60% — present but not dominant. This inverts the BMW pattern, where BEV growth (i4, iX, iX3) was the sharpest signal. For Audi's Portugal digital funnel, the primary commercial question is whether the Q3 configurator experience is optimised for ICE/hybrid buyers, not whether BEV configurator completion rates are adequate.

**2. The BEV story is declining, not growing**

BMW's 2024 BEV share grew to 17.4% (+13.5% YoY). Audi's fell to ~9.3% (−19.5% in production). The commercial implication for digital measurement is the opposite: Audi does not have a high-growth BEV segment demanding configurator investment. The digital priority should follow the demand signal — which is the Q3 and the broader ICE/hybrid portfolio.

**3. `importrust` at +140% is a conversion leakage signal**

A third-party import platform appearing in Audi's rising purchase-intent queries means some high-intent buyers are exiting the official funnel entirely. This leakage is structurally invisible to Audi's own analytics. The measurement question is not just "what is our configurator completion rate?" but "what share of buyers who researched on audi.pt never entered our funnel at all?" Only external search signal analysis — not GA4 — can surface this.

**4. The digital strategy gap vs. BMW**

BMW publicly commits triple-digit million annual investment in digital sales and frames the configurator as a conversion tool. Audi's Annual Report does not. This gap may reflect actual investment differences, strategic communication differences, or both. Either way, the observable signals — configurator framed as specification tool, no online purchase path referenced — suggest the digital funnel's role in Audi's sales model is less explicitly defined than BMW's. This shapes what the funnel model observation should look for: whether the configurator ends in a lead CTA or merely in a dealer handoff with no direct digital conversion path.
