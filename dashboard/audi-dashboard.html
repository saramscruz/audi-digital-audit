What this means for Audi Portugal

Positions taken
01

The Q3 has the demand — the funnel only has a dealer form

The Q3 is at +2,000% on YouTube Portugal and BREAKOUT in web search. The configurator on configurador.audi.pt is genuinely low-friction: 5 steps, price visible throughout from step 1, lead form requiring only name and contact preference — 3 fields, well below the conversion drop-off threshold. The problem is not the form. It is what the form leads to: a dealer handoff, nothing else. No online reservation. No way to act on completed intent outside dealer hours. BMW offers an online reservation path on the i4. Audi offers none on the Q3.

02

The re-engagement mechanism is outsourced to a third party

Saving a Q3 configuration requires a carLOG account — a portal outside Audi's own ecosystem. The 6.3× return-visitor lift means high-intent buyers who save and return are the most valuable cohort in the funnel. Audi is depending on a third-party account creation to capture them. An email-only save option — no account required — removes that barrier at the cost of one sprint.

03

Some buyers are not reaching the funnel at all

importrust at +140% in Audi's rising purchase-intent queries in Portugal signals that a measurable share of high-intent buyers is routing to grey-market import channels before engaging with audi.pt. This leakage is invisible to GA4 — it does not show up in any internal analytics report. It only appears in search signals. The question is not how to convert these users; they have already converted somewhere else.

04

Re-engagement windows are too short

Converters take a median of 3 sessions and 129 hours from first visit to purchase. A standard 24-hour retargeting window discards the majority of eventual buyers before they convert. This is a campaign settings change — not a creative problem — and costs nothing to test. For Audi specifically, where the carLOG save barrier suppresses return visits, extending the re-engagement window is the higher-priority lever.

05

Fix CPC mobile landing pages, not mobile broadly

Mobile referral already outperforms desktop (7.53% vs 7.01%). The CPC gap — mobile 4.28% vs desktop 5.06% — is where the problem concentrates. The Q3 configurator on configurador.audi.pt is functionally identical on desktop and mobile (observed June 2026): current step name visible, full sequence accessible via dropdown tap. The friction is upstream, in the paid landing experience before the configurator is reached.

Priority recommendation

What changes with real Audi GA4 access

Every position above is directionally valid but inferred from proxy data and public signals. With access to Audi's analytics backend: the Q3's exact configurator entry rate by device and channel, the return visit rate for carLOG savers vs non-savers, the mobile CPC gap in basis points for Q3 specifically, and session count for configurator abandoners. Audi is already collecting this data. Running these queries takes hours.

Three testable product hypotheses

Sprint-sized decisions, falsifiable success metrics
H1

Measure Q3 configurator entry rate vs the rest of the model range

The Q3 is generating BREAKOUT search demand. The open question is whether that demand is entering the configurator, or dissipating on the model page before it gets there. Proposed analysis: segment configurator_start rate by model, isolating Q3. If Q3's entry rate is lower than the portfolio average despite higher search interest, the model page CTA is the fix — not the configurator. Success metric: configurator entry rate for Q3 vs all other models, segmented by device and channel. Cost: one BigQuery query against Audi's GA4 export.

H2

An email-only save option will outperform carLOG account creation for re-engagement

The 6.3× return-visitor lift makes the save mechanism the highest-value lever in the funnel. carLOG requires creating an account on a third-party portal. Proposed change: add an email-only "save and continue later" option at the configurator summary screen, requiring no account. Send a single re-engagement email at 48 hours and 5 days if the configuration is not completed. Success metric: return visit rate for email-save users vs carLOG users vs non-savers. Secondary: lead submission rate within 14 days for each group. Cost: one sprint for the save prompt; email automation via existing CRM.

H3

A dedicated mobile landing page for Q3 CPC traffic will close the paid mobile gap

Mobile CPC converts at 4.28% vs desktop 5.06%. Mobile referral outperforms desktop at 7.53% vs 7.01%. The Q3 configurator is functionally identical on both devices — the friction is upstream, in the paid landing experience before configurador.audi.pt is reached. Proposed change: a dedicated mobile landing page for Q3 paid search campaigns, loading the configurator directly at Step 1 (Modelo) with the Q3 pre-selected, bypassing the model range page. Surface the 3-field lead form as an alternative CTA for users not ready to configure. Success metric: CPC conversion rate on mobile. Baseline: 4.28%. Target: parity with desktop CPC (5.06%) within 60 days. Cost: one sprint. No configurator changes required.
