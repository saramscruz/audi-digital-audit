What this means for Audi Portugal

Positions taken
01

The Q3 has the demand — the funnel ends at a dealer form

BREAKOUT in Portuguese web and YouTube search — “audi q3 sportback 2026” BREAKOUT, “audi q3 2026” +700% web and +600% YouTube. Observed Google Trends PT, 26 June 2026. The configurator on configurador.audi.pt has 6 tabs, price visible from Step 1 (€48,251), and “Disponível Imediatamente — 2 Carros novos disponíveis imediatamente” visible while configuring. Stock exists. The problem is what the configurator leads to: “Solicitar uma proposta” — a dealer form, nothing else. BMW presents “Reservar” at Step 12 of 12 on the i4 configurator. At the end of the Q3 configurator (‘O meu Audi’ tab), the only available CTA is “Solicitar uma proposta” — a dealer form. No Reserve. No online order. The Audi Code (e.g. E7HJY6) provides a shareable URL but is not surfaced as a primary action. “Disponível Imediatamente — 2 Carros novos disponíveis imediatamente” is visible in the Exterior tab while configuring. Stock exists with no digital path to act on it. Observed bmw.pt 12 June 2026 and configurador.audi.pt 26 June 2026.

What amplifies the gap

Re-engagement windows are too short

Cox Automotive 2024 Car Buyer Journey Study (n=2,310, published January 2025): new-vehicle buying requires 13.5+ hours of active research across an average of 4.1 websites. A standard 24-hour retargeting window discards the majority of eventual buyers before they convert. This is a campaign settings change — not a creative problem — and costs nothing to test. For Audi specifically, where the carLOG save barrier suppresses return visits, extending the re-engagement window is the higher-priority lever.

Fix CPC mobile landing pages, not mobile broadly

Mobile referral already outperforms desktop (1.76% vs 1.70%). The CPC gap — mobile 0.97% vs desktop 1.09% — is where the problem concentrates. The Q3 configurator on configurador.audi.pt is functionally identical on desktop and mobile (observed 26 June 2026): current tab name visible, full sequence accessible via dropdown. The friction is upstream, in the paid landing experience before the configurator is reached.

02

The re-engagement mechanism is outsourced to a third party

Saving a Q3 configuration requires creating an account on carLOG — a third-party portal outside Audi’s own ecosystem. The save modal warns that configurations may expire if the product range changes. No email-only option exists. Observed configurador.audi.pt, 26 June 2026. The 51.5× return-visitor lift (BigQuery verified, proxy-analysis.sql) means high-intent buyers who save and return are the most valuable cohort in the funnel. Audi is depending on a third-party account creation to capture them. An email-only save option — no account required — removes that barrier at the cost of one sprint.

03

Some buyers are not reaching the funnel at all

importrust generates equivalent search interest in Portugal to “audi q3” itself — Google Trends index 52 vs 51, past 12 months. Source: Google Trends PT, web search, 26 June 2026. This demand is invisible to GA4 — it does not show up in any internal analytics report. It only appears in search signals. Within the Audi Q3 search cluster, BYD appears as a BREAKOUT rising query — a conquest signal that buyers researching the Q3 are also researching BYD. The question is not how to convert importrust users; they have already converted somewhere else.

Priority recommendation

What changes with real Audi GA4 access

Every position above is directionally valid but inferred from proxy data and public signals. With access to Audi’s analytics backend: the Q3’s exact configurator entry rate by device and channel, the return visit rate for carLOG savers vs non-savers, the mobile CPC gap in basis points for Q3 specifically, and session count for configurator abandoners. Audi is already collecting this data. Running these queries takes hours.

Three testable product hypotheses

Sprint-sized decisions, falsifiable success metrics
H1

Measure Q3 configurator completion rate vs the model range

The Q3 is generating BREAKOUT search demand and has stock available immediately. The open question is whether that demand is completing the configurator, or abandoning before the end screen. Proposed analysis: segment configurator completion rate by model, isolating Q3. If Q3’s completion rate is lower than the portfolio average despite higher search interest, the end screen CTA — not the configurator — is the fix. Success metric: lead submission rate at “O meu Audi” for Q3 vs all other models, segmented by device. Cost: one BigQuery query against Audi’s GA4 export.

H2

An email-only save option will outperform carLOG account creation for re-engagement

The 51.5× return-visitor lift (BigQuery verified, proxy-analysis.sql) makes the save mechanism the highest-value lever in the funnel. carLOG requires creating an account on a third-party portal with an expiry warning. Proposed change: add an email-only “save and continue later” option at the configurator summary screen, requiring no account. Send a single re-engagement email at 48 hours and 5 days if the configuration is not completed. Success metric: return visit rate for email-save users vs carLOG users vs non-savers. Secondary: lead submission rate within 14 days for each group. Cost: one sprint for the save prompt; email automation via existing CRM.

H3

A dedicated mobile landing page for Q3 CPC traffic will close the paid mobile gap

Mobile CPC converts at 0.97% vs desktop 1.09%. Mobile referral outperforms desktop at 1.76% vs 1.70%. The Q3 configurator is functionally identical on both devices — the friction is upstream, in the paid landing experience before configurador.audi.pt is reached. Proposed change: a dedicated mobile landing page for Q3 paid search campaigns, loading the configurator directly at the Linha de equipamento tab with the Q3 pre-selected, bypassing the model range page. Surface the lead form as an alternative CTA for users not ready to configure. Success metric: CPC conversion rate on mobile. Baseline: 0.97%. Target: parity with desktop CPC (1.09%) within 60 days. Cost: one sprint. No configurator changes required.
